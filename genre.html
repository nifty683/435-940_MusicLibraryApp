Genres

Here is a list of genre options in the music library, and a brief description of each.

Blues

Blues music is most closely related to folk music. The biggest difference is that blues music feature more sad melodies and lyrics. Blues originated in the Deep South as work songs, chants, and narrative ballads.

Classical

Classical music is generally Western orchastral arrangements, featuring large symphonies and usually lacking in lyrics.

Country

Country music focuses on blue-collar American life. It often utilizes simple string instruments, such as guitars, fiddles, banjos.

Electronic

Electronic music is typically upbeat dance music that has been heavily processed on a computer.

Folk

Folk music is a rural type of music similar to blues and country. These songs are simple and are passed down for generations.

Hip-hop

Hip-hop, or rap, is a kind of music that utilizes a strong rhythmic beat, and intense vocal rhythm.

Jazz

Jazz is a kind of ragtime music that often utilizes very warm instruments, like pianos, saxaphones, trumpets, etc. Improvization is very important in jazz.

New Age

New age music is a type of ambient music meant more to be a sort of background or lead in relaxation.

Reggae

Reggae music is typically slower, with accents on the offbeat. This creates a sort of relaxed bounce.

Rock

Rock is a form of music with an intense and strong beat/track. It utilizes intense drums, electric guitars, bass, and vocals.
